What this app does: every morning L.A.B.A.N. reads the market news, checks your Robinhood account, and turns it all into simple report cards — so you can see what deserves your attention without wading through jargon.

The score (0–100): how strong a stock looks right now. 80+ = A (strong), 65–79 = B (decent), 50–64 = C (so-so), under 50 = D/F (weak — be careful).

Every card shows both sides: the best reasons the price could go UP and the best reasons it could go DOWN. Never just the hype.

MODEL LEAN: L.A.B.A.N.'s overall tilt, as a percent. "65% bullish" means leaning positive — a lean, not a promise.

LEVELS: prices worth watching. Support is a floor where buyers stepped in before; resistance is a ceiling where sellers showed up.

Dotted words: anything underlined like this is trader-speak — tap it for a plain-English meaning.

Remember: this is research, not financial advice. Nothing here trades for you — you are always the pilot.
